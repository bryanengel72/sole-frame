Setup

Two things stand between you and your first post.

Connect your Notion to Claude, then learn how to talk to your engine. Both take under ten minutes, and you only do this once.

01 Connect Notion
02 Start requesting posts
01

Connect your Notion.

Your engine's tracking system — duplicate protection, your pillar map, everything it remembers about what you've published — lives in a private Notion tracker we set up for you and share to your account. It's yours to see and use, and no other client can see it. Claude needs permission to read and write to it, and that's what a Connector does.

01

Have a Notion account

Free at notion.so. Sign up if you don't already have one — that's all you need. You won't build anything; the account just gives us somewhere to send your tracker.

02

Accept your tracker invite

We create your private tracker in our workspace and invite your Notion account to it as an editor. Accept the invite — it arrives by email and in Notion — and the tracker appears in your sidebar under Shared. It's already built, with your pillars and duplicate protection ready to go.

03

Open Connectors in Claude

In Claude, go to Settings → Connectors. On mobile, it's under the same Settings menu. This is a list of outside tools Claude can plug into — Notion is one of them.

04

Find Notion and connect it

Search Notion in the connector directory and select Connect. You'll be sent to Notion's own site to sign in and approve access — Claude never sees your Notion password, and you're approving the connection on Notion's page, not Claude's.

05

Give Claude access to your tracker

Notion will ask which pages to share with Claude. Make sure your Sole Frame tracker — the one we shared in step 02 — is included. That's the only page the engine needs to reach.

06

Confirm it worked

Back in your conversation with Claude, say the line below. If the connection is live, Claude will confirm it can see your tracker before doing anything else.

"Confirm you can see my Sole Frame Notion tracker."

02

Start your engine.

Your engine is installed as a Claude skill, tuned to your voice, your city, and your ideal clients during onboarding. Once it's live, you never open a dashboard or fill out a form. You just tell Claude what you want.

Who sets it up
We tune the engine to your voice and market before you ever touch it. This step is already done by the time you're reading this — you're just learning how to use it.
Where you use it
Any conversation with Claude where your engine is available. No separate app, no login beyond Claude itself.
What it never does
Publish anything without you seeing it first. Every post stops at an approval gate — nothing reaches Notion, let alone your website, until you say go.

Requesting your first post

Ask in plain language. The engine figures out the pillar, checks for duplicates against everything you've already published, and writes to your voice — not a generic template.

If you know what you want

"Write a blog post about on-location headshots for law firms in [your city]."

If you don't

"What should I write about this week?"

After a post is approved

"Generate the Google Business Profile posts for that one too."

Checking your progress

"Show me my pillar map — what am I missing?"

What comes back

01

A full blog post in your voice, with SEO metadata, five title options, and suggested image names.

02

The full content pack — LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, email, and an FAQ block — generated automatically.

03

Three Google Business Profile post variants, each with a suggested image name.

04

A chance to ask for changes before anything gets logged, as many rounds as you need.

05

Once approved: logged to Notion, tracked against your pillar map, remembered forever.
