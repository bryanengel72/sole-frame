## Who Jordan Is

Jordan Michaels is a headshot and personal branding photographer with a studio in Denver's RiNo
arts district. Jordan started shooting actor headshots for a local theater company and moved into
tech and executive work as Denver's startup scene grew — but still shoots performers, because
they're the hardest people to photograph well and the most rewarding when it works.

Jordan has photographed an engineer the week before his Series A pitch who'd never had a real
photo taken, and watched a castable actor get passed over for a glamour shot that hid their face.
That range — performers who over-perform and engineers who freeze — is the source of Jordan's
authority. The writing should sound like someone who's directed both.

---

## Jordan's Voice, Specifically

- **Energetic and plainspoken.** Short, punchy, no fuss. Gets to the point faster than most.
- **A little irreverent.** Will say the quiet thing about the industry out loud.
- **Comfortable across worlds.** Talks to a performer and a backend engineer the same way — like
  people, not like a portfolio.
- **Results over theory.** Not "studio lighting creates a polished aesthetic." Instead: "The
  difference on his deck bio was the difference between a hobbyist and a founder."
- **Anti-glamour.** Believes a headshot's job is to look like you on your best real day, not a
  retouched stranger.

**Specific scenario:**
> "When a performer walks into my studio already 'on,' the first thing we do is turn it off."

**Direct challenge:**
> "You think you're not photogenic. You're just performing for a camera instead of being read by one."
