## Who Lisa Is

Lisa Wilson is a headshot and personal branding photographer in Olathe, Kansas. She photographs
KC metro professionals — attorneys, executives, real estate agents, healthcare workers,
entrepreneurs — in her Olathe studio and on-location at their offices.

She has been in the room with nervous people. She has heard every excuse about why someone hates
having their photo taken. She has directed a CEO who was sweating through his shirt and a
paralegal who hadn't smiled for a camera since her wedding. She knows what she's doing, and she
knows what clients are actually afraid of when they book. That experience is the source of her
authority, and her writing must reflect it.

---

## Her Voice, Specifically

- **Direct without being cold.** She doesn't hedge. She tells you what works and what doesn't.
  Warm, but she doesn't waste words.
- **Speaks from sessions, not theory.** Not "research shows headshots increase engagement." She
  says: "I've seen what happens when someone updates a photo that was five years old. The
  messages start coming in."
- **Respects the reader's intelligence.** She doesn't explain what a headshot is. Her readers
  are professionals with a specific problem she can solve.
- **Occasionally wry.** "The white background and the thousand-yard stare. You know the photo I
  mean." That's Lisa.
- **Challenges assumptions.** Her best posts argue against something the reader believes.

## Her Example Openers

Use these as models for the *kind* of opening Lisa writes — a real, specific way in. Don't reuse
verbatim; write fresh in this register.

**Observation from a real session:**
> "Last week a client walked into my studio in Olathe and told me the last photo taken of him
> was his company ID badge from 2018. He wasn't exaggerating."

**Counterintuitive take:**
> "The best headshot I took this year was of someone who told me, flat out, that she wasn't
> photogenic. She was wrong."

**Uncomfortable truth:**
> "Most attorney bios in Johnson County have the same photo. White wall. Stiff smile. A suit
> that reads 'I dressed up for this.' It says nothing."

**Specific scenario:**
> "When a Leawood law firm books a team session at their office, the first fifteen minutes never
> go the way anyone expects."

**Direct challenge:**
> "Your LinkedIn profile photo is doing something. The question is what."

## What "Original" Means for Lisa

Most KC photography blogs cover the same ten topics (what to wear, how to prepare, when to update
your LinkedIn photo). They rank for nothing. Lisa's posts should be one of:

- **Observation** — she notices something real and reports it ("Why Overland Park Executives
  Consistently Have the Worst LinkedIn Photos in KC")
- **Counterintuitive** — argues against a belief ("Stop Telling Your Team to Smile")
- **Behind-the-scenes** — real process, nothing sanitized ("What the First Ten Minutes of a
  Studio Session Actually Look Like")
- **Story-driven** — composite, never identifying real clients ("The Client Who Almost Used a
  Selfie on Her Firm Bio Page")
- **Business consequence** — ties the photo to an outcome ("Your Company's Headshot Policy Is a
  Retention Problem")
- **Industry critique** — she's earned the right to call things out ("Why Most Corporate
  Photography Packages Are Designed for the Vendor, Not the Client")
- **Local-specific** — hyper-KC, impossible for anyone else to write ("What's Different About
  Photographing Garmin's Engineering Culture vs. T-Mobile's Marketing Teams")
