# A/B Title Variants (Shared Engine)

Every post gets five alternate titles alongside the main SEO title, in the metadata block.
The client picks one, or tests two. Examples below use [City]/[Audience] placeholders — fill
from client-profile.md.

---

## Why Five Variants

The title that ranks isn't always the one that gets clicked, shared, or opened:
- Search rewards specificity and keyword match
- LinkedIn rewards curiosity gaps and counterintuitive angles
- Email subject lines reward story hooks and direct benefits
- Facebook rewards questions and emotional resonance
- Returning visitors reward authority and credibility signals

Five variants gives one entry point per channel without writing five posts.

---

## The Five Approaches

Generate exactly one per approach. Label each.

### 1. Curiosity
Withholds just enough to create a need to know. Can't be answered from the title alone.
- Creates a tension the reader wants to close; the post must resolve it
- Best on LinkedIn and email; avoid worn-out "You won't believe..." formats
> "The One Thing [City] HR Managers Get Wrong Before Every Team Headshot Session"

### 2. Contrarian
Argues against something the reader believes. Creates good friction.
- Be specific about the conventional wisdom being challenged; the post must deliver
- Best for thought-leadership and social sharing
> "Why Smiling Is the Worst Direction You Can Give Your Team Before Headshots"

### 3. Benefit-led
Leads with the outcome. Pure clarity, no mystery.
- The benefit must be specific, not "better headshots"; keep keyword + city present
- Best in search and for readers close to a decision
> "How [City] Corporate Teams Get Natural-Looking Headshots Without Coaching Their Expressions"

### 4. Story hook
Opens a narrative or a character. The reader wants to know what happens.
- Introduces a person/situation/before-after; character need not be named ("a [Suburb] partner")
- Story must be real or composite — no manufactured drama; best for email subject lines
> "The [Suburb] Attorney Who Kept Cancelling Her Headshot — and What Finally Changed"

### 5. Authority / most specific
The most precise, credible, information-dense version. Signals deep expertise.
- Specific numbers, roles, locations; keyword + city front and center
- Best in search and for high-intent readers comparing providers
> "What 200 Headshot Sessions in [County] Taught Me About Corporate Photo Direction"

---

## Generation Rules

1. All five keep the primary keyword or a natural semantic variant.
2. City name appears in at least three of the five.
3. No two variants use the same angle.
4. None is clickbait — every title delivers on its promise.
5. Keep under 75 characters where possible; flag anything longer.

---

## Delivery Format

```
TITLE: [Main SEO-optimized title]

A/B TITLE VARIANTS:
1. [Curiosity] "[title]" -- best for: LinkedIn, email
2. [Contrarian] "[title]" -- best for: social sharing, thought leadership
3. [Benefit-led] "[title]" -- best for: search, high-intent readers
4. [Story hook] "[title]" -- best for: email subject line, social caption
5. [Authority] "[title]" -- best for: search, readers comparing providers
```

---

## Choosing Between Variants

| If the post is going... | Recommend |
|---|---|
| Primarily on LinkedIn | Curiosity or Contrarian |
| As an email newsletter | Story hook (subject) + Benefit-led (preview) |
| To rank in Google | Authority or Benefit-led |
| To be shared on social | Contrarian or Story hook |
| To a warm audience (existing list) | Story hook or Curiosity |
| To a cold audience (paid/organic search) | Benefit-led or Authority |

The main title stays on the website. A variant can be the social headline, email subject, or
LinkedIn opener — same post, multiple entry points.
