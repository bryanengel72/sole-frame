# Content Pack Reference (Shared Engine)

Every blog post generates a full content pack automatically — no extra prompting. The pack
always follows the blog post in the same output. Read voice-rules.md and voice-persona.md
first: every piece must sound like the photographer, not a social template or a newsletter bot.

Contains, in this order: (1) social posts — LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook; (2) email newsletter
with subject lines; (3) FAQ block for the bottom of the post; (4) image brief.

---

## 1. Social Posts

### LinkedIn
- 150–250 words, first person throughout
- Opens with a hook or observation — never "I just published a new post!"
- One insight from the post told from a slightly different angle — a companion piece, not a summary
- **No hashtags** (they read as spam on LinkedIn to a professional audience)
- Ends with a soft invite; include `[Link to post]` at the end
- Check: would the photographer say this to a peer at a networking event?

### Instagram
- 100–200 word caption, warmer and more behind-the-scenes than LinkedIn
- References a visual moment from a studio or on-location session
- 18–22 hashtags at the bottom, separated by a line break
- Ends with "Link in bio to read more" or a booking nudge
- Lead with `[Suggested image: ...]` describing what from the portfolio pairs with it
- **Build the hashtag set from client-profile.md**: local (city + suburbs + county), headshot/
  craft tags, and audience tags (the clusters in profile §6). Mix broad + local + niche.

### Facebook
- 100–200 words, conversational, slightly less formal than LinkedIn
- Can speak directly to a booking decision ("if you've been putting this off...")
- Ends with a question to drive comments; weave `[Link to post]` in naturally; no hashtags

## 2. Email Newsletter

A self-contained email that stands alone and links to the full post.

**Subject lines — 3 options, distinct approaches:**
| Approach | Does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Curiosity | Withholds just enough | "The thing I never say before a session starts" |
| Direct benefit | States the payoff | "How to prep your team for headshots (and what not to tell them)" |
| Story hook | Opens a narrative | "She almost used a selfie on her firm's bio page" |

- Under 50 characters where possible; sentence case; no emojis unless the brand uses them

**Preview text:** one line, 80–100 characters, extends the subject without repeating it.

**Body:** 200–300 words. Hook opening, one core insight (leave more for the link), short
paragraphs, one link placed naturally, CTA. Sign off with the photographer + business + location
(from profile §1–2).

---

## 3. FAQ / Featured Snippet Block

Pastes into the bottom of the post. Formatted for AEO — questions phrased the way someone types
or speaks them to Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, or a voice assistant.

- **4–6 questions for clusters, 5–7 for pillars** (canonical table in SKILL.md)
- Each answer 2–4 sentences, self-contained, no fluff
- At least one question includes the city name and primary keyword naturally
- H3 per question; label the section "## Common Questions" (not "FAQ")
- Don't restate what the post already covered — add value

---

## 4. Image Brief

Tells the photographer which portfolio shots to use and how to label them for SEO.

- **2–3 images for a cluster, 3–5 for a pillar**
- Each gets: description, alt text, file name, optional caption
- **Alt text:** 120–150 chars, descriptive of what's in the photo, primary keyword included
  naturally — not keyword-stuffed
- **File names:** lowercase, hyphens only, keyword-rich, numbered (e.g. `attorney-headshot-[city]-01.jpg`)

## Content Pack Voice Audit

- [ ] LinkedIn opens with a hook (not "I just published...")
- [ ] Instagram references a real visual moment from a session
- [ ] Facebook invites engagement
- [ ] All three sound like the photographer, not a scheduler
- [ ] Email subject earns the open without clickbait
- [ ] Email body stands alone — valuable without clicking
- [ ] FAQ questions match how a real person would ask them; answers self-contained
- [ ] Image brief gives enough to find the right shot; alt text descriptive, not stuffed
- [ ] No banned words from voice-rules.md
